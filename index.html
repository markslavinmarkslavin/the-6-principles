The Framework

Six Principles

Instead of "trying harder," this approach asks: what specifically is missing? What would need to be true for this problem to dissolve?

I

Data

What evidence already exists? Ground every analysis in observable facts before introducing theory.

II

Existing

What frameworks already work? Leverage what has been proven — don't rebuild from nothing.

III

Bottleneck

What is the ONE thing that fails? Identify the single constraint that makes everything else stuck.

IV

Concrete

What exact condition must hold? Vagueness is the enemy — define the success criterion with precision.

V

Continuum

How does it behave at limits? Test the edges — where does the model break, and what does that reveal?

VI

Spectral

What is the bridge to the solution? Map the path from current state to resolved state in discrete steps.

How It Works

The App Crashes

Problem

The application fails under certain conditions — but "fix the bug" is not an actionable directive.

Bottleneck

Not the symptom. Find the exact input sequence that triggers failure — the precise condition that must hold for the crash to occur.

Once the triggering condition is known exactly, the solution space collapses from infinite to one.

Which Job Should I Take?

Problem

A decision between opportunities — but "compare salaries" answers the wrong question.

Bottleneck

Identify what actually constrains you: financial security, intellectual growth, location, or meaning? Only then does comparison become useful.

Once the true constraint is named, the "difficult decision" becomes obvious.

The methodology identifies what specifically must be proven — not the proof itself.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Is this free?
Yes. The methodology is open and free. The tools (problem_solver.js, self-improvement-engine.js) are open source under MIT License.
How long does it take?
The bottleneck analysis takes 5–15 minutes. The full 6 Principles process: 1–2 hours for complex problems with sufficient data.
Does it work for personal problems?
Yes. Career, relationships, health — any problem where you are "trying hard but not making progress" is a bottleneck problem. The framework applies wherever a constraint can be named.
What is the success rate?
The benchmark shows 86% composite score on self-optimization tasks. For external problems, outcomes depend on the quality and completeness of available data.
