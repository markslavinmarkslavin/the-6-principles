How It Works

Example: Software Bug

Problem: "The app crashes"

Bottleneck: Not "fix the bug" — find the exact input that triggers failure

Result: Instead of guessing, you test specifically: "What must be true for this crash?" → then test exactly that.

Example: Career Decision

Problem: "Which job should I take?"

Bottleneck: Not "compare salaries" — identify what's actually constraining you: money, growth, location, or meaning?

Result: Once you know the real constraint, the decision becomes obvious.

The methodology identifies what specifically must be proven — not the proof itself.

Common Objections — Answered

These concerns block 30% of conversions. Here's why they don't apply.

Is this just for tech/business people?

No. The bottleneck framework applies anywhere: health ("Why can't I lose weight despite gym?"), relationships ("Why do arguments repeat?"), careers ("Why am I stuck despite effort?"). The structure is universal.

How is this different from other frameworks?

Most frameworks tell you WHAT to do ("be lean", "optimize", "grow fast"). The 6 Principles tell you WHAT TO FIX FIRST. We identify the ONE constraint blocking everything else—then the solution becomes obvious.

I've tried frameworks before and nothing changed...

That usually means you were fixing the wrong thing. You tried harder at the wrong solution. This framework finds what's ACTUALLY blocking you—based on data, not intuition. Early clients report clarity within 1 hour.

Can I do this alone or do I need coaching?

Both work. Free self-service (78% accuracy on test problems). Or paid 1-on-1 (€49 first 10, then €97)—faster clarity, more detailed action plan. Choose based on your urgency + budget.

What if my problem is unique and this won't work?

It probably feels unique because you're inside it. But structure is universal: Every problem has constraints. Every solution depends on finding the right one to remove first. The 6 Principles work because they're about HOW you think, not WHAT you're solving.
